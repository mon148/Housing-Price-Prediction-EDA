## Housing Price Prediction  
  
### Exploratory Data Analysis Phase  
I have uploaded the analysis and inspection phase of this project where I disclosed the inconsistenciess, null, grouping of the colmns and also how they were systematically individually dealt with and not globally dealt with.
After data has been fully inspected and cleaned, there remamin the understanding of:  
- Feature behavior  
- Relationships  
- Distribution  
- Anomalies  
- Predictive relevance  
- Target Variable analysis  
- Distribution Analysis  
  
This is the reason come onto building a separate project which shows the understanding of these. Instead of globally building in same workspace to directly dive into the modelling part which, if data is not adequatrely and accurately understood might give a bias prediction.  
  
The target variables (Salesprice) showed a positive skewness and afetr proper observation, it was detected to being greater than `1.0`. This observation and the result disclose:
- Extreme high-values properties existed  
- Linear assumption may become unstable  
- Outliers present
- And lots more which would be disclose futher in this documentation.  
  
Observation showed that some large properties had price inconsistency and this observation indicated:  
- Potential Luzury market anomalies
- Data entry inconsistencies  
- Non-linear market behavior  
  
All these are what indicated the Presence of outliers. These outliers were cautiously handled in order to aoid removing legitimate premium market properties  
  
Some numerical variables showed strong relationship with housing prices. Some of the target-variable-high-impact include:  
- Overall quality  
- Garage capacity  
- Basement size  
- Living area  
- Construction year  
  
There were some key observaations deduced from this:  
- Higher-quality homes consistently sold at premium prices  
- Larger living areas strongly increased valuation  
- Newer properties generally commanded higher prices.  
  
Some categorical variables analysis also identified pricing separation accross:   
- Neighborhoods  
- Zoning classes  
- Quality ratings  
- Kitchen quality  
- Sale conditions.  
This price separation entails:  
- Rare occurrences  
- Sparse representation   
- Limited predictive reliability.  
**This findings ensures future encoding strategies**  
  
Correlation analysis also revealed strong positive relationships between size-related variables and
overlapping explanatory behavior among garage and room-related features.  
Some potential multi-colinearity risk were detected between:  
- garage area vs garage capacity,
- total rooms vs living area,
- basement area vs first-floor area.  
This relationship informed feature-selection decision.  
  
Some findings justify future transformation decision because multiple variables exhibited non-normal distribution. Some of these variables include:  
- SalePrice  
- LotArea  
- GrLivArea. 
This finding disclosed some potential risks:  
- Unstable linear coefficients  
- Inflated variance  
- Sensitivity to extreme values.  
  
These observation, findings and patterns revealed practical real-estate insights:   
- Quality drives more than quantity: Construction quality influence prices more than mere strongly than pure sixe metrics.  
- Location remains a major catalyst that drives price: Neighbourhood significantly separate premium and lower-value properties  
- Extreme luzury properties behave differently: very large homes do not always follow market pricing behavior. And this is why some large square-feet which were over `4000sqft` resulted into outliers and thereby attracted proper handling.  
- Some features contribute minimal predictive value: certain outdoor and miscellaneous features exhibited weak relationships with price thereby, showing weak correlation with the saleprice. ## Housing Price Prediction
